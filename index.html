About

I am a PhD candidate with a recently submitted thesis (July 2025) in Computational Psychological Neuroscience from the Neural Systems and Behaviour Lab under the supervision of Alex Fornito

My research focused on enhancing the validity and reliability of brain-behavior predictive models by developing novel approaches to feature engineering in both functional neural networks and latent behavioral spaces. Using a multidisciplinary toolkit including machine learning, network/graph theory, structural equation modeling, and advanced statistical techniques (dimensionality reduction, time-series analysis, and spectral analysis), I established robust frameworks for high-dimensional prediction problems.

This work has given me expertise in building interpretable predictive models where feature quality is paramount. I'm particularly passionate about developing machine learning systems where engineered features (whether derived from domain knowledge, statistical transformations, or network representations) create the foundation for reliable inference.

With my PhD research completed, I'm now excited to apply my analytical expertise to data science/applied science roles. My experience transforming complex, noisy datasets into meaningful insights has given me both the technical skills and the problem-solving mindset to deliver impactful, data-driven solutions. I'm particularly drawn to opportunities where rigorous computational methods can solve real-world challenges. My background in developing statistically robust models for neuroscience applications translates directly to business contexts where data quality and methodological soundness is essential to determine success.

If my skills align with your team's needs, I'd welcome the chance to discuss how I could contribute to your projects. Please feel free to reach out via email or LinkedIn.
